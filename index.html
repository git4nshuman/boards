Mock Test System — Strategy
📅

When to Start Mocks

Phase 2 (Day 81+): Start with subject-wise mocks (1 subject at a time). Full 300-mark mocks only from Phase 3 (Day 156+).

📊

Frequency

Phase 2: 1 subject mock per week. Phase 3: Full mock every 2 days. Last 2 weeks: daily mock.

🔍

Analysis Method

After every mock: categorize each wrong answer as (1) Silly mistake, (2) Concept gap, (3) Never studied. Tackle each differently.

📈

Score Improvement System

If score drops: don't panic — take 1 buffer day, then revisit the 3 weakest chapters from that mock. Never attempt 2 full mocks back to back without analysis.

🎯

Target Scores

90 percentile ≈ 120–140 marks. 95 percentile ≈ 155–170. 98 percentile ≈ 190–210. 99 percentile ≈ 220–240.

⚠️

Negative Marking

Attempt only if 70%+ confident. Skip if unsure. Each wrong = −1 in JEE Main (NTA). Never guess on a topic you haven't studied.

PRIORITY RULES

What to do when behind, overloaded, or uncertain.

If Behind Schedule

🔴 Emergency Protocol

1. Don't try to catch up everything at once.
2. Mark skipped tasks — they auto-reschedule in the plan.
3. Prioritize: Chemistry inorganic first (fastest marks), then Maths (formula recall), then Physics.
4. Cut any topic not in PYQ top-10 frequency list.

🟡 Safe to Temporarily Skip

- Complex proof derivations
- Problems harder than PYQ Level 3
- New topics in Phase 3 (skip entirely — only revision)
- Optional practice sheets beyond 30 questions/chapter

🟢 Minimum Viable Day (MVD)

On bad days, this is enough:
• 20 PYQs (any subject) — 45 min
• Formula revision — 15 min
• 1 chapter flashcard read — 10 min
Total: 70 minutes. You're still in the game.

Time Wasting Traps — DO NOT DO
❌

Watching long lecture videos

Max 30min theory video per topic. Notes > videos. PYQs > videos.

❌

Rewriting neat notes instead of revising

Use rough notes or digital notes. Rewriting from scratch = time sink.

❌

Doing too many books

1 book per subject + PYQs. Don't hop between HC Verma, DC Pandey, Irodov, etc.

❌

Solving mocks without analysis

If you're not spending equal time on analysis, mocks are useless.

❌

Perfectionism paralysis

"I'll start after I finish this topic perfectly" — this kills preparation. Move forward even with gaps.

What Separates 98%ile Students
✅

Consistent revision cycles

A chapter revised 4 times beats one studied once in 8 hours.

✅

PYQ-first approach

JEE Main repeats patterns. Know what has come. That alone = 70% of marks.

✅

Error log discipline

Every wrong answer logged and revisited. That's how weak points die.

BURNOUT PROTOCOL

Recovery systems for bad days, anxiety spirals, and focus loss.

Burnout Day Protocol
😮‍💨

Minimum Output Day (MOD)

Do only: 20 PYQs + formula flash (30 min). No guilt. No extra. Tomorrow is a new day.

📵

Digital Detox Mode

Close all study apps. Read only printed notes. No YouTube, no Reddit. 1 hour walk outside.

🛌

Sleep First Policy

If you're at below 6h sleep, studying is counterproductive. Sleep is revision. Period.

Anxiety Overload Handling
🧠

The "One Task" Rule

When everything feels overwhelming: open your plan, pick ONE task (the smallest one), complete it. That's it. Momentum builds from 1.

📊

Progress Check Reality

Open your analytics page. See actual hours logged. Often anxiety comes from losing track of real progress. Data beats feelings.

Lost Focus Recovery System
⏱

2-Minute Start Rule

"I'll study for just 2 minutes." Open the book. Start. 90% of the time you continue for much longer. Starting is the hardest part.

📍

Environment Reset

Change your study spot. Different room, different desk, even a library. Novel environments force focus.

🎧

Use the Focus Timer

Use 25-min Pomodoro mode. You're not studying for 3 hours — you're studying for 25 minutes. That's it.

Restart After 2–3 Bad Days
🔁

The Reset Protocol

1. Don't try to compensate everything at once.
2. Mark all skipped tasks in the plan (they reschedule).
3. Start fresh from today — 1 chapter, 20 PYQs.
4. 200 days is long. 3 bad days is 1.5%. You're fine.

💬

Self-Talk Reset

"I haven't failed. I paused. Today I restart." Say this. Write it. Mean it. JEE is a 200-day game, not 3 perfect days in a row.

EXAM STRATEGY

What to do in the exam hall — attempt order, time, guessing, negative marking.

Why this order? Chemistry is fastest (memory-based) → builds confidence. Maths next when brain is fresh. Physics last (calculations, needs focus but also comfort from earlier confidence).

Time Allocation
🧪

Chemistry — 35 minutes

30 MCQ + 10 numerical. Target: complete all in 35 min. Don't spend more than 1.5 min per MCQ.

📐

Maths — 55 minutes

Hard section. Attempt the easy ones first. Skip and mark hard ones. Come back if time allows.

⚡

Physics — 50 minutes

Mixed: some pure theory (easy) + calculations (medium/hard). Attempt theory first within Physics.

Guessing Strategy & Negative Marking
🎯

When to attempt uncertain questions

Attempt ONLY if you can eliminate 2 options (50/50 chance). At 50/50: expected value is 0 — slightly worth it.

🚫

When to skip

If all 4 options seem possible = skip. If topic is from unprepared area = skip. Protecting your score > chasing 4 marks.

🔢

Numerical questions

No negative marking on NTA numericals! Always attempt these even if unsure. Put your best calculated guess.

🧘

Mindset in exam

If a question feels hard: circle, move on, come back. Never spend 5+ min on one question. Time = marks in JEE Main.
